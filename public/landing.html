What it does

Built for footballers,
not gym-goers.

Most training apps treat football as an afterthought. With Vector, the match schedule is the foundation and everything else fits around it.

Periodised to your fixture list

Sessions shift based on how many days you are from a match. Heavy gym work happens early in the week. Lighter activation closer to matchday. It adjusts when your schedule changes.

Test yourself. Train to your numbers.

10m, 30m, CMJ, RSA, Yo-Yo. Run the testing battery and Vector uses your actual scores to set loads. Not a percentage of some average player's numbers.

Works with what you've got

Full gym, basic gym, or just a park. Tell it your access and the programme adapts. Change it any time. Your plan updates for the new situation.

Progressive overload, done for you

Sets, reps, intensity, and rest periods progress across the weeks automatically. You don't need to plan it. Just show up and do the session.

How it works

Set up in five minutes.

Three things, then you're training.

1
Tell it your situation

Position, play style, training days, match day, gym access. Takes about two minutes. The more accurate you are, the better the programme.

2
Run the fitness tests (optional)

Sprint times, jump height, aerobic fitness. Optional but it makes the programme significantly more accurate for your current level.

3
Follow the plan

Your full programme is generated instantly. Log sessions in the app, track progress, and update your schedule whenever fixtures change.
